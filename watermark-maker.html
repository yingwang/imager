Why Use Watermarks?

Watermarks serve as a visual copyright notice that identifies you as the creator of an image. They deter unauthorized use by making it clear that the image is protected intellectual property. Professional photographers, designers, and content creators use watermarks on preview images shared online, while delivering unwatermarked versions only to paying clients.

An effective watermark should be visible enough to identify the owner but subtle enough not to distract from the image itself. Semi-transparent text placed diagonally across the image, or a small logo in a corner, are common approaches. SnapTools lets you control the text, font size, color, position, opacity, and rotation of your watermark to achieve the exact look you want.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I add a logo watermark instead of text?

Currently, SnapTools Watermark Maker supports text-based watermarks with customizable font, size, color, opacity, and rotation. Logo watermark support is planned for a future update. For now, you can use your brand name or copyright notice as a text watermark.

Where should I place my watermark?

The best placement depends on your goal. A center or diagonal watermark provides maximum protection against unauthorized use. A corner watermark is less intrusive and works well for portfolio previews. SnapTools lets you choose from multiple positioning options and adjust the exact placement to suit your image.

Will the watermark reduce image quality?

The watermark is rendered directly onto your image using the HTML5 Canvas API, so the output quality is equivalent to the original. The watermark text is rasterized at the image's native resolution, ensuring crisp, clean text regardless of the image size.
